Has any elite Seattle pro athlete ever had a longer goodbye than Earl Thomas?

It feels like the day when he would officially become an ex-Seahawk has been building since Christmas Eve 2017, when he infamously approached Cowboys coach Jason Garrett outside the Dallas locker room and told him to “come get me” once the Seahawks kicked him to the curb.

That day has finally arrived.

But despite the often messy (Thomas telling the Seahawks they are number one in his heart as he was carted off the field at Arizona) and far-too-drawn-out ending, Thomas will always be a Seahawk.

He finishes his Seattle career with six Pro Bowl nods, tied with Russell Wilson for fourth-most in a Seahawks uniform behind three players who all have since made the Hall of Fame — Walter Jones, Cortez Kennedy and Steve Largent. Thomas was also named a first team All-Pro three times, behind only Jones and Bobby Wagner, who each have four as Seahawks.

As we say a final goodbye to Thomas, a look back at his five most memorable plays as a Seahawk.
